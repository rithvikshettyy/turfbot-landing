# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Turf and sports-venue owners in India — often non-technical, running a 5/7-a-side football ground, cricket box, or multi-sport facility. They manage bookings manually over phone calls and WhatsApp, lose slots to no-shows and missed messages, and want more confirmed bookings without hiring staff or learning new software. They arrive on a phone, between matches, skeptical and short on time. The job to be done: "Stop leaking revenue from missed and disorganized bookings — let an automated WhatsApp system capture and confirm them for me."

## Product Purpose

TurfBook is a landing page selling an automated WhatsApp booking system for turf owners: instant slot confirmation, UPI payment links, reminders, real-time availability, and monthly reports. The page's job is to convert a skeptical owner into a WhatsApp lead. Success = the owner understands the revenue they're losing, trusts the system to be reliable, and taps the WhatsApp CTA to start setup.

## Brand Personality

A blend of four traits, in priority order: **trustworthy → simple → sporty → premium**. Trust leads because owners stake their revenue pipeline on this. Simple because the audience is non-technical and time-poor. Sporty for category energy and momentum. Premium for the polished finish that justifies the price. Voice: confident, plain-spoken, benefit-led, zero jargon. Three words: **reliable, effortless, sharp**. Emotional driver: **FOMO / urgency** — sell against revenue lost to missed bookings, but keep urgency honest and grounded, never spammy.

References that capture the right feel:
- **Linear / Vercel** — engineered precision, tight type, premium confidence. The "this product is solid" signal.
- **Nike / sports brands** — athletic energy, bold confident imagery, momentum. The category-fit spark.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered template-marketplace landing pages.
- Generic stock-corporate blandness.
- Dark/aggressive "gamer" aesthetics.
- Over-animated AI-slop gradients and decorative glassmorphism.
- Anything that reads as hype or undercuts trust (fake countdowns, fear-mongering).

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before flash** — every visual choice must make an owner feel safe putting their bookings here. Polish serves credibility, not decoration.
2. **Urgency, honestly** — frame around real revenue lost to missed bookings; make the cost of doing nothing visible without spam or fake scarcity.
3. **One-thumb simple** — mobile-first, plain language, obvious next step. A busy non-technical owner grasps the value and reaches the CTA in seconds.
4. **WhatsApp is the hero path** — the green WhatsApp action is the consistent, unmistakable primary CTA everywhere; reduce every flow to "chat to get started."
5. **Show the work, don't shout** — credibility through clear product explanation (how it works, real benefits), not louder copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast on all text (body ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1), including placeholder text.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: disable reveal/parallax-style motion, fall back to crossfade or instant.
- Semantic structure, alt text, and ARIA labels for screen-reader users.
- Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states on nav, CTAs, and the lead form.
